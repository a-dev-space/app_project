Signed Numbers (Positives/Negatives)

  • Algebraic subtraction: \(a-b = a+(-b)\).
  • Add/Subtract rules: Same signs ‚Üí keep sign & add magnitudes. Different signs ‚Üí subtract smaller magnitude from larger, keep sign of larger magnitude.
  • Multiply/Divide rules: Same signs ‚Üí positive; different ‚Üí negative. Thus ‚Äúeven # of negatives ‚Üí +, odd ‚Üí ‚àí‚Äù.

Opposites & Absolute Value

  • Opposite: Numbers equally distant from 0 on the number line have opposite signs (e.g., 4 and ‚àí4). 0 is its own opposite.
  • Absolute value: distance from 0; always non-negative. Evaluate inside first: e.g., \(|-3-4| = |-7| = 7\); in expressions, simplify then take absolute value.

Divisibility, Multiples, Primes

  • Divisibility test summary:
    • 2: last digit even; 3: sum of digits divisible by 3; 4: last two digits divisible by 4; 5: last digit 0 or 5; 6: divisible by 2 and 3; 7: \(5\times\)last digit + rest divisible by 7 (or rest ‚àí \(2\times\)last digit divisible by 7); 8: last three digits divisible by 8; 9: sum of digits divisible by 9; 10: last digit 0.
  • Multiples: numbers of the form \(k\cdot n\) for whole \(k\). 0 is a multiple of every number; division by 0 is never allowed.
  • Prime vs Composite: Prime >1 divisible only by 1 and itself (e.g., 7, 11). Composite >1 with ‚â•1 other divisor (e.g., 6, 21).
  • Prime factorization (‚Äúproduct of primes‚Äù): break into primes; write with exponents, e.g., \(12=2^2\cdot 3\); \(45=3^2\cdot 5\).
  • GCF (greatest common factor): intersection of prime powers at minimum exponents (e.g., GCF(14,28)=14).
  • LCM (least common multiple): union of prime powers at maximum exponents (e.g., LCM(20,75)=\(2^2\cdot 3\cdot 5^2=300\)).

Fractions

  • Concept: part-of-a-whole; numerator ‚Äúparts taken‚Äù, denominator ‚Äúequal parts total‚Äù. Percent means ‚Äúper-hundred‚Äù (e.g., \(76\%=\frac{76}{100}\)).
  • Equivalent fractions (Denominator‚ÄìNumerator Rule): multiplying top & bottom by same non-zero \(c\) leaves value unchanged: \(\frac{a}{b}=\frac{ac}{bc}\) (rationalize denominators via this). Signs: you may flip any two signs (numerator/denominator/out-front) without changing value.
  • Reciprocal: \(a\ne0 \Rightarrow \text{reciprocal}=\frac1a\); \(a\cdot \frac1a=1\).
  • Division by zero: denominator 0 ‚Üí undefined; numerator 0 ‚Üí value 0.
  • Simplify to lowest terms: cancel common prime factors of numerator/denominator; if equal ‚Üí fraction equals 1. (Worked example \(630/945\to 2/3\)).
  • Add/Subtract:
    • Like denominators: \(\frac{a}{d}\pm\frac{b}{d}=\frac{a\pm b}{d}\).
    • Unlike denominators: use LCD (LCM of denominators), convert to equivalent fractions, then add/subtract; reduce. (Worked example \(5/24+3/40\)).
  • Ratio & Proportion: ratio compares two quantities; a proportion asserts two ratios are equal.
  • Percent markup: \(\text{Selling Price}=\text{Purchase Price}+\text{Markup}\).

Exponents

  • Exponential notation: \(x\cdot x\cdot\ldots\cdot x = x^n\).
  • Laws (for real \(a,b\), base \(x\ne0\)):
    • Product: \(x^a x^b = x^{a+b}\), and \((xy)^a = x^a y^a\).
    • Power: \((x^a)^b = x^{ab}\).
    • Quotient: \(\dfrac{x^a}{x^b} = x^{a-b}\).
    • Negative exponents: \(x^{-n}=\dfrac{1}{x^n}\).
    • Special: \(1^n=1\) for all \(n\).

Radicals

  • Definitions: square root \((\sqrt{x})^2=x\) for \(x>0\); cube root \((\sqrt[3]{x})^3=x\) (sign can be ¬±); fourth root \((\sqrt[4]{x})^4=x\) (principal root positive). Terms: radical sign \( \sqrt{\phantom{x}} \), radicand (inside), index (2 if omitted).
  • Product of square roots: for non-negative \(m,n\): \(\sqrt{m}\sqrt{n}=\sqrt{mn}\) and \(\sqrt{mn}=\sqrt{m}\sqrt{n}\). If both negative, \(\sqrt{m}\sqrt{n}=-\sqrt{mn}\).
  • Simplified radical form: no perfect-square factor in the radicand and no radicals in denominator.
